# Preface

This volume arranges the Gospel material into a single narrative sequence while preserving the distinct details that can stand together without contradiction. Repetition is reduced, parallel scenes are merged, and the wording is recast into a unified voice for continuous reading.

Used well, a harmony like this can help readers follow the full movement of Jesus' life and ministry without repeatedly leaving one account to assemble another. It can make the narrative arc easier to trace, help major speeches and turning points stand in relation to one another, and give a clearer sense of sequence, development, and cumulative force across the four Gospels.

Two presentation modes are anticipated in this project. The review edition retains paragraph-level source references for editorial verification. The reader edition suppresses those references in the visible text so that the work reads more like a continuous book.

The text of this harmony is newly written prose based on the source passages identified in the references. It should not be treated as a substitute for reading the canonical Gospels themselves.
