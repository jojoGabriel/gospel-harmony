# Section Prompt Template

Use this template when drafting or revising one section of the harmonized Gospel.

## Prompt

Write the section titled `[SECTION TITLE]` for a harmonized Gospel narrative.

### Goal

Produce a single, condensed Gospel account from the relevant passages in Matthew, Mark, Luke, and John. The result should read as if written by one author, in one continuous voice.

### Source Passages

- Matthew: `[PASSAGES]`
- Mark: `[PASSAGES]`
- Luke: `[PASSAGES]`
- John: `[PASSAGES]`

### Requirements

- Arrange events in chronological order.
- Merge parallel accounts smoothly.
- Remove repetition and duplicate storytelling.
- Preserve all meaningful details that can be included without clutter.
- Use readable but not modernized English.
- Follow a meaning-for-meaning approach guarded by formal accuracy.
- Do not add concepts, motives, or explanations not found in the text.
- Keep the prose natural, dignified, and Scripture-shaped.
- In teachings and discourses, preserve the concrete images, sayings, and contrasts of the passage rather than turning them into abstract summary.
- In major speeches, prophecies, and testimony scenes, prefer expanded harmony that allows the speech to stand more directly rather than reducing it to third-person report.
- Do not include verse numbers in the narrative body.
- Append paragraph-level source references after each paragraph in this form: `<span class="review-ref">[Matt 1:18-25]</span>`.

### Output Format

1. A title line
2. A references line
3. The harmonized prose for the section, with `.review-ref` paragraph references

### Revision Checks

- Does this section read smoothly as one narrative?
- Are any repeated sayings or scenes unnecessarily duplicated?
- Is the wording faithful to the source meaning?
- Does it read like a close retelling of the text rather than commentary about the text?
- In speech-heavy paragraphs, does it sound like the speaker is actually speaking rather than being summarized?
- Does the tone match the established style guide?
- Are any chronology decisions noted for `references.md`?
