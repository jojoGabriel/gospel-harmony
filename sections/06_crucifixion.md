# Crucifixion

References: Matthew 26-27; Mark 14-15; Luke 22-23; John 13-19

When the Passover and the Feast of Unleavened Bread were near, the chief priests and scribes were seeking a way to seize Jesus by stealth and kill Him, though they feared the people. Then Satan entered Judas Iscariot, one of the Twelve. He went away to the chief priests and captains and spoke with them about how he might deliver Jesus to them. They were glad and agreed to give him money, and from that time he looked for an opportunity to hand Him over apart from the crowd. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 26:1-5, 14-16; Mark 14:1-2, 10-11; Luke 22:1-6]</span>

On the first day of Unleavened Bread, when the Passover lamb was being prepared, Jesus sent Peter and John ahead, telling them where they would find a furnished upper room made ready. They did as He said and prepared the Passover. When the hour came, He reclined at table with the apostles and said, "I have greatly desired to eat this Passover with you before I suffer." <span class="review-ref">[Matt 26:17-20; Mark 14:12-17; Luke 22:7-16]</span>

During supper, Jesus knew that the Father had given all things into His hands, and that He had come from God and was going to God. He rose from the table, laid aside His outer garment, girded Himself with a towel, and began to wash the disciples' feet. When He came to Peter, Peter protested, but Jesus said, "If I do not wash you, you have no share with Me." After He had washed them all, He said, "I have given you an example. The servant is not greater than his master." <span class="review-ref">[John 13:1-17]</span>

Then Jesus was troubled in spirit and said, "Truly I tell you, one of you will betray Me." The disciples looked at one another in grief and uncertainty, and each began to ask whether he was the one. Jesus indicated the betrayer by giving him a morsel. After Judas received it, Jesus said, "What you are going to do, do quickly." Judas went out at once, and it was night. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 26:21-25; Mark 14:18-21; Luke 22:21-23; John 13:18-30]</span>

While they were eating, Jesus took bread, gave thanks, broke it, and gave it to them, saying, "Take and eat; this is My body, which is given for you. Do this in remembrance of Me." In the same way He took the cup after supper and gave it to them, saying, "This cup is the new covenant in My blood, poured out for many for the forgiveness of sins." He also said, "I will not again drink of the fruit of the vine until the kingdom of God comes." <span class="review-ref">[Matt 26:26-29; Mark 14:22-25; Luke 22:17-20]</span>

A dispute also arose among them about which of them should be considered greatest. But Jesus said, "The kings of the nations rule over them; it must not be so among you. Let the greatest among you become as the youngest, and the leader as the one who serves." After Judas had gone out, He said, "A new command I give you: love one another. Just as I have loved you, so you also are to love one another. By this all men will know that you are My disciples." <span class="review-ref">[Luke 22:24-30; John 13:31-35]</span>

Jesus said to them, "Do not let your hearts be troubled. Trust in God; trust also in Me. In My Father's house there is room enough, and I am going to prepare a place for you. I am the way, and the truth, and the life. If you love Me, keep My commandments, and I will ask the Father, and He will give you another Helper to remain with you. Peace I leave with you; My peace I give to you. Do not let your hearts be shaken, and do not be afraid." <span class="review-ref">[John 14:1-31]</span>

Again He said, "I am the true vine, and My Father is the vinegrower. Remain in Me, and I in you. As the branch cannot bear fruit by itself unless it remains in the vine, so neither can you unless you remain in Me. This is My commandment, that you love one another as I have loved you." He warned them that the world would hate them as it had hated Him, yet promised that the Helper, the Spirit of truth, would bear witness concerning Him. He said, "When He comes, He will guide you into all the truth." And He told them, "You will have sorrow, but your sorrow will be turned into joy." <span class="review-ref">[John 15:1-16:33]</span>

Lifting His eyes to heaven, Jesus prayed and said, "Father, the hour has come. Glorify Your Son, that the Son may glorify You. I have manifested Your name to those whom You gave Me out of the world. Keep them in Your name. Sanctify them in the truth; Your word is truth. I do not ask for these only, but also for those who will believe through their word, that they all may be one. Father, I desire that those whom You have given Me may be with Me where I am, to see My glory." <span class="review-ref">[John 17:1-26]</span>

When they had sung a hymn, they went out to the Mount of Olives. Jesus said, "All of you will stumble this night, for the shepherd will be struck and the sheep scattered." Peter answered, "Even if all others stumble, I will not." But Jesus said to him, "Truly, before the rooster crows, you will deny Me three times." Yet Peter insisted that he would die with Him rather than deny Him, and the others said the same. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 26:30-35; Mark 14:26-31; Luke 22:31-38; John 13:36-38]</span>

Then they came to a place called Gethsemane. Jesus took Peter, James, and John farther with Him, and He began to be greatly distressed. He said, "My soul is overwhelmed with sorrow, even to death. Remain here and keep watch." Going a little farther, He fell on His face and prayed that if it were possible the hour might pass from Him; yet not His will, but the Father's, was to be done. An angel from heaven strengthened Him. He came and found the disciples sleeping, and again and again He warned them to watch and pray, that they would not enter into temptation. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 26:36-46; Mark 14:32-42; Luke 22:39-46]</span>

While He was still speaking, Judas arrived with a crowd carrying swords and clubs from the chief priests and elders. Judas came near to greet Him with a kiss, but Jesus said, "Judas, would you betray the Son of Man with a kiss?" When Jesus stepped forward and said, "I am He," they drew back and fell to the ground. Then they laid hands on Him. Simon Peter drew a sword and struck the high priest's servant, cutting off his right ear, but Jesus rebuked him, touched the servant, and healed him. Then He yielded Himself into their hands. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 26:47-56; Mark 14:43-52; Luke 22:47-53; John 18:1-12]</span>

They led Jesus first to Annas, and then to Caiaphas the high priest, where the scribes and elders were assembled. False witnesses came forward, yet their testimony did not agree. At last the high priest put Him under oath and asked whether He was the Christ, the Son of the Blessed One. Jesus answered, "You will see the Son of Man seated at the right hand of power and coming with the clouds of heaven." Then they said He had spoken blasphemy, and they mocked Him, struck Him, and covered His face. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 26:57-68; Mark 14:53-65; Luke 22:54, 63-71; John 18:13-14, 19-24]</span>

Peter had followed at a distance into the courtyard. There he was recognized again and again as one of Jesus' disciples. But he denied it before them all. After the third denial, immediately a rooster crowed. Then the Lord turned and looked at Peter, and Peter remembered His word. He went out and wept bitterly. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 26:69-75; Mark 14:66-72; Luke 22:54-62; John 18:15-18, 25-27]</span>

When morning came, Judas saw that Jesus had been condemned, and he was filled with remorse. He brought back the silver to the chief priests and elders, saying that he had sinned by betraying innocent blood. But they refused to take responsibility. So Judas threw the silver into the sanctuary, departed, and ended his life. The priests used the money to buy the potter's field as a burial place for strangers. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 27:3-10]</span>

Then they led Jesus from Caiaphas to the governor's headquarters. They did not enter, so that they might remain clean for the Passover. Pilate went out to them and then brought Jesus inside for questioning. He asked, "Are You the King of the Jews?" Jesus answered, "My kingdom is not from this world." Though Pilate found no fault worthy of death in Him, the priests pressed their accusations. Learning that Jesus was from Galilee, Pilate sent Him to Herod, who mocked Him and sent Him back arrayed in splendor. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 27:1-2, 11-14; Mark 15:1-5; Luke 23:1-12; John 18:28-38]</span>

Now at the feast it was customary to release one prisoner. The crowd, stirred up by the chief priests, asked for Barabbas, a man imprisoned for murder in the insurrection. Pilate wanted to release Jesus, but they cried out that He should be crucified. Pilate took water and washed his hands before the crowd, declaring himself innocent of this blood. Then he released Barabbas to them, but delivered Jesus to be scourged. The soldiers clothed Him in scarlet and purple, set a crown of thorns upon His head, placed a reed in His hand, struck Him, and mocked Him, saying, "Hail, King of the Jews." <span class="review-ref">[Matt 27:15-31; Mark 15:6-20; Luke 23:13-25; John 18:39-19:3]</span>

Pilate brought Him out and said, "Behold the Man." But when the chief priests and officers saw Him, they cried, "Crucify Him." When they insisted that He had made Himself the Son of God, Pilate grew more afraid. Yet because the uproar was rising and they declared that anyone who made himself a king opposed Caesar, Pilate sat down on the judgment seat and finally handed Jesus over to be crucified. Pilate wrote a title and fixed it to the cross, naming Him Jesus of Nazareth, the King of the Jews. <span class="review-ref">[John 19:4-16, 19-22]</span>

As they led Him away, they seized Simon of Cyrene and laid the cross upon him to carry behind Jesus. A great multitude followed, including women who mourned for Him. But Jesus turned to them and told them not to weep for Him, but for themselves and for their children. When they came to the place called Golgotha, they offered Him wine mingled with bitterness, but He would not drink it. There they crucified Him, and with Him two others, one on either side and Jesus in the middle. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 27:32-38; Mark 15:21-28; Luke 23:26-33; John 19:17-18]</span>

Jesus said, "Father, forgive them, for they do not know what they are doing." The soldiers divided His garments and cast lots. Those who passed by reviled Him, and the rulers mocked Him. One of the criminals joined in the abuse, but the other rebuked him and said, "Jesus, remember me when You come into Your kingdom." Jesus answered him, "Truly I tell you, today you will be with Me in paradise." Standing near the cross were His mother, His mother's sister, Mary the wife of Clopas, Mary Magdalene, and the disciple whom He loved. Seeing them, Jesus committed His mother to that disciple's care. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 27:39-44; Mark 15:29-32; Luke 23:34-43; John 19:23-27]</span>

From about the sixth hour until the ninth, darkness came over all the land. Jesus cried out with a loud voice, "My God, My God, why have You forsaken Me?" Knowing that all had now been accomplished, He said, "I thirst." After receiving the sour wine, He cried out again, "It is finished." Then He said, "Father, into Your hands I commit My spirit," and He bowed His head and gave up His spirit. At that moment the curtain of the temple was torn, the earth shook, rocks were split, and the centurion, seeing what had happened, glorified God and confessed that truly this Man was righteous and truly the Son of God. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 27:45-56; Mark 15:33-41; Luke 23:44-49; John 19:28-30]</span>

Because it was the day of preparation, the Jews asked that the bodies not remain on the crosses during the Sabbath. The soldiers broke the legs of the two crucified with Him, but when they came to Jesus they saw that He was already dead. Instead one of them pierced His side with a spear, and immediately blood and water came out. The crowds went away beating their breasts, while the women who had followed Him from Galilee remained at a distance, watching these things. <span class="review-ref">[John 19:31-37; Luke 23:48-49]</span>

When evening had come, Joseph from Arimathea, a rich man and respected member of the council who was himself waiting for the kingdom of God, went boldly to Pilate and asked for the body of Jesus. Nicodemus also came, bringing a mixture of myrrh and aloes. They took the body, wrapped it in linen with the spices according to the burial custom, and laid Him in Joseph's own new tomb in a garden near the place of crucifixion. The women saw the tomb and how His body was laid, then returned and prepared spices and ointments, and on the Sabbath they rested according to the commandment. The next day the chief priests and Pharisees secured the tomb with a seal and set a guard over it. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 27:57-66; Mark 15:42-47; Luke 23:50-56; John 19:38-42]</span>
