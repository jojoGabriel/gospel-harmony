# John the Baptist and Beginning of Ministry

References: Matthew 3-4; Mark 1; Luke 3-4; John 1-4

In the fifteenth year of Tiberius Caesar, when Pontius Pilate governed Judea, Herod ruled Galilee, Philip ruled Iturea and Trachonitis, and Lysanias ruled Abilene, during the high priesthood of Annas and Caiaphas, the word of God came to John son of Zechariah in the wilderness. He went through the whole region around the Jordan, proclaiming a baptism of repentance for the forgiveness of sins, according to what was written in Isaiah the prophet about a voice crying in the wilderness to prepare the way of the Lord and make His paths straight. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 3:1-3; Mark 1:1-4; Luke 3:1-6]</span>

John wore clothing of camel's hair and a leather belt around his waist, and his food was locusts and wild honey. Jerusalem and all Judea, and all the district around the Jordan, were going out to him. They were baptized by him in the Jordan, confessing their sins. But when he saw many of the Pharisees and Sadducees coming for baptism, he said, "Brood of vipers, who warned you to flee from the wrath that is coming? Bear fruit worthy of repentance, and do not begin to say within yourselves, 'We have Abraham as our father.' God is able from these stones to raise up children for Abraham. Already the axe lies at the root of the trees. Every tree that does not bear good fruit is cut down and thrown into the fire." <span class="review-ref">[Matt 3:4-10; Mark 1:6; Luke 3:7-9]</span>

The people asked him, "What then should we do?" He answered, "Whoever has two tunics must share with the one who has none, and whoever has food must do likewise." Tax collectors also came and asked, "Teacher, what should we do?" He said, "Take no more than has been appointed." Soldiers also asked, "And we, what should we do?" He answered, "Do not extort, do not accuse falsely, and be content with your pay." Since the people were in expectation and all were wondering in their hearts whether John might be the Christ, he answered them all, saying, "I baptize you with water, but One stronger than I is coming, and I am not worthy even to loosen the strap of His sandals. He will baptize you with the Holy Spirit and with fire. His winnowing fork is in His hand to clear His threshing floor, gather the wheat into His barn, and burn the chaff with unquenchable fire." So John exhorted the people in many ways and preached good news to them. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 3:11-12; Mark 1:7-8; Luke 3:10-18]</span>

This is the witness John gave when the Jews sent priests and Levites from Jerusalem to ask who he was. He confessed openly, "I am not the Christ." When they asked whether he was Elijah, he said, "I am not." When they asked whether he was the Prophet, he answered, "No." Then he said, "I am the voice of one crying in the wilderness, 'Make straight the way of the Lord.'" When they asked why he baptized if he was none of these, John answered, "I baptize with water, but One already stands among you whom you do not know, the One coming after me, whose sandal I am not worthy even to loosen." <span class="review-ref">[John 1:19-28]</span>

When the people were receiving baptism, Jesus also came from Nazareth in Galilee to the Jordan to be baptized by John. John tried to prevent Him, saying, "I need to be baptized by You, and do You come to me?" But Jesus answered, "Permit it now, for in this way it is fitting for us to fulfill all righteousness." Then John consented. After Jesus was baptized and had come up from the water praying, heaven was opened. The Spirit of God came down upon Him like a dove, and a voice came from heaven, saying, "This is My beloved Son, in whom I am well pleased." Jesus was about thirty years old when He began. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 3:13-17; Mark 1:9-11; Luke 3:21-23]</span>

John also bore witness, saying, "I saw the Spirit descend from heaven like a dove and remain on Him. I myself did not know Him, but the One who sent me to baptize with water told me, 'The Man on whom you see the Spirit descend and remain, this is the One who baptizes with the Holy Spirit.' I have seen and have borne witness that this is the Son of God." <span class="review-ref">[John 1:32-34]</span>

At once the Spirit led Jesus out into the wilderness, where for forty days He was tempted by the devil. He ate nothing through those days, and when they ended, He was hungry. The tempter said to Him, "If You are the Son of God, command these stones to become bread." Jesus answered, "Man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word that comes from God." Then the devil took Him to the holy city and set Him on the pinnacle of the temple, saying, "If You are the Son of God, throw Yourself down, for it is written that He will command His angels concerning You, and that they will bear You up." Jesus replied, "It is also written: 'You shall not put the Lord your God to the test.'" Again the devil showed Him all the kingdoms of the world and their glory in a moment, and said, "All these I will give You if You will fall down and worship me." But Jesus said to him, "Go, Satan, for it is written: 'You shall worship the Lord your God, and Him only shall you serve.'" After every temptation had ended, the devil left Him for a time, and angels came and ministered to Him. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 4:1-11; Mark 1:12-13; Luke 4:1-13]</span>

The next day John saw Jesus coming toward him and said, "Behold, the Lamb of God, who takes away the sin of the world. This is the One of whom I said, 'After me comes a Man who has gone before me, because He was before me.'" The following day John was standing with two of his disciples, and when he saw Jesus walking by, he again said, "Behold, the Lamb of God." The two disciples heard this and followed Jesus. One of them was Andrew, Simon Peter's brother. He first found Simon and told him, "We have found the Messiah," that is, the Christ, and he brought him to Jesus. Jesus looked at him and said, "You are Simon son of John. You will be called Cephas," which means Peter. <span class="review-ref">[John 1:29-42]</span>

The next day Jesus decided to leave for Galilee. He found Philip and said, "Follow Me." Philip then found Nathanael and said, "We have found the One written about by Moses in the Law and by the prophets: Jesus of Nazareth, Joseph's son." Nathanael asked, "Can anything good come out of Nazareth?" Philip said, "Come and see." When Jesus saw Nathanael approaching, He said, "Behold, a true Israelite in whom there is no deceit." Nathanael asked, "How do You know me?" Jesus answered, "Before Philip called you, when you were under the fig tree, I saw you." Nathanael answered, "Rabbi, You are the Son of God. You are the King of Israel." Jesus said, "You will see greater things than these. You will see heaven opened and the angels of God ascending and descending upon the Son of Man." <span class="review-ref">[John 1:43-51]</span>

On the third day there was a wedding at Cana in Galilee, and Jesus' mother was there. Jesus and His disciples were also invited. When the wine was gone, His mother said to Him, "They have no wine." Though His hour had not yet come, He told the servants to fill the stone jars with water. After they had filled them completely, He said, "Draw some out now and take it to the master of the feast." When the master tasted it, the water had become wine, though only the servants knew where it had come from. He called the bridegroom and said, "Every man sets out the good wine first, and afterward the poorer. But you have kept the good wine until now." By this first sign at Cana, Jesus manifested His glory, and His disciples believed in Him. <span class="review-ref">[John 2:1-11]</span>

After this He went down to Capernaum with His mother, His brothers, and His disciples, and they remained there a few days. When the Passover approached, Jesus went up to Jerusalem. In the temple courts He found men selling oxen, sheep, and doves, and money changers seated at their tables. Making a whip from cords, He drove them all out with the sheep and cattle, scattered the money changers' coins, and overturned their tables. He told those selling doves to take these things away and stop making His Father's house a house of trade. His disciples remembered the Scripture that says zeal for God's house would consume Him. <span class="review-ref">[John 2:12-17]</span>

The Jewish leaders then asked, "What sign do You show us, seeing that You do these things?" Jesus answered, "Destroy this temple, and in three days I will raise it up." They replied, "This temple has been forty-six years in building, and will You raise it up in three days?" But He was speaking about the temple of His own body. So after He rose from the dead, His disciples remembered that He had said this and believed both the Scripture and His word. <span class="review-ref">[John 2:18-22]</span>

While He was in Jerusalem at the Passover feast, many believed in His name when they saw the signs He was doing. But Jesus did not entrust Himself to them, because He knew all men and had no need for anyone to testify concerning man, for He knew what was in man. <span class="review-ref">[John 2:23-25]</span>

There was a Pharisee named Nicodemus, one of the Jewish rulers, who came to Jesus by night and said, "Rabbi, we know that You have come from God as a teacher, because no one could do these signs unless God were with him." Jesus answered him, "I tell you truly, unless a man is born from above, he cannot see the kingdom of God." Nicodemus asked, "How can a man be born when he is old? Can he enter his mother's womb a second time and be born?" Jesus answered, "Unless a man is born of water and Spirit, he cannot enter the kingdom of God. What is born of flesh is flesh, and what is born of the Spirit is spirit. Do not marvel that I said to you, 'You must be born from above.' The wind blows where it wills, and you hear its sound, but you do not know from where it comes or where it goes. So it is with everyone born of the Spirit." Nicodemus said, "How can these things be?" Jesus answered, "Are you the teacher of Israel and yet you do not understand these things? As Moses lifted up the serpent in the wilderness, so the Son of Man must be lifted up, so that everyone who believes in Him may have eternal life. For God loved the world in this way: He gave His only Son, so that everyone who believes in Him should not perish but have eternal life. God did not send His Son into the world to condemn the world, but that the world might be saved through Him. But the Light has come into the world, and men loved the darkness rather than the Light, because their deeds were evil." <span class="review-ref">[John 3:1-21]</span>

After this Jesus and His disciples went out into the Judean countryside and spent time there, and His disciples were baptizing. John too was baptizing at Aenon near Salim, because there was much water there. When a dispute arose about purification, John's disciples came and said, "Rabbi, the One who was with you beyond the Jordan, to whom you have borne witness, behold, He is baptizing, and all are going to Him." John answered, "A man receives nothing unless it has been given him from heaven. You yourselves can testify that I said, 'I am not the Christ, but I have been sent before Him.' The one who has the bride is the bridegroom. But the friend of the bridegroom stands and hears him, and rejoices greatly because of the bridegroom's voice. So this joy of mine is now full. He must increase, but I must decrease. The One who comes from above is above all. Whoever believes in the Son has eternal life." <span class="review-ref">[John 3:22-36]</span>

When Jesus learned that the Pharisees had heard He was gathering and baptizing more disciples than John, though Jesus Himself did not baptize, but His disciples did, He left Judea. And after Herod had imprisoned John for his rebukes, Jesus returned to Galilee in the Spirit's power, and word about Him spread through the surrounding country. From then on He proclaimed, "The time is fulfilled, and the kingdom of God has drawn near. Repent and believe the good news." <span class="review-ref">[John 4:1-3; Matt 4:12-17; Mark 1:14-15; Luke 4:14-15]</span>
