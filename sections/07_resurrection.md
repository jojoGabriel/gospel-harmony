# Resurrection

References: Matthew 28; Mark 16; Luke 24; John 20-21

After the Sabbath, while it was still very early on the first day of the week, Mary Magdalene and the other women came to the tomb carrying the spices they had prepared. There had been a great shaking, for an angel of the Lord had come down and rolled away the stone. When they arrived, they found the stone already moved and the tomb open. Entering, they did not find the body of the Lord Jesus. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 28:1-4; Mark 16:1-4; Luke 24:1-3; John 20:1]</span>

While they stood in perplexity, messengers in dazzling clothing appeared and said, "Why do you seek the living among the dead? He is not here; He has been raised. Remember how He told you while He was still in Galilee that the Son of Man must be delivered into the hands of sinful men, be crucified, and on the third day rise." Fear fell upon the women, yet they remembered His words and ran from the tomb with trembling and great joy to tell the disciples. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 28:5-8; Mark 16:5-8; Luke 24:4-9]</span>

Mary Magdalene ran ahead and came to Simon Peter and the other disciple whom Jesus loved. She said, "They have taken the Lord out of the tomb, and we do not know where they have laid Him." Then Peter and the other disciple ran to the tomb. The other disciple arrived first and saw the linen cloths lying there. Peter went in and saw the cloths and the face cloth folded separately. Then the other disciple also went in, and he saw and believed. Yet they still did not understand from the Scripture that He had to rise from the dead. So the disciples returned to their homes. <span class="review-ref">[Luke 24:12; John 20:2-10]</span>

But Mary remained outside the tomb weeping. As she stooped to look in, she saw two angels seated where Jesus' body had lain. They asked, "Woman, why are you weeping?" She answered, "They have taken away my Lord, and I do not know where they have laid Him." Turning around, she saw Jesus standing there, though she did not recognize Him. He said to her, "Woman, why are you weeping? Whom are you seeking?" Supposing Him to be the gardener, she asked where the body had been laid. Jesus said to her, "Mary." She turned and said, "Rabboni." Jesus said to her, "Do not cling to Me, for I have not yet ascended to the Father. But go to My brothers and tell them that I am ascending to My Father and your Father, to My God and your God." Mary Magdalene came and announced to the disciples, "I have seen the Lord," and told them what He had said to her. <span class="review-ref">[John 20:11-18]</span>

As the other women were on their way, Jesus met them and greeted them. They came and took hold of His feet and worshiped Him. Then Jesus said, "Do not be afraid. Go and tell My brothers to go to Galilee, and there they will see Me." <span class="review-ref">[Matt 28:9-10]</span>

Some of the guard also went into the city and reported to the chief priests all that had happened. After gathering with the elders, they gave a large sum of money to the soldiers and told them to say that His disciples came by night and stole Him away while they slept. So that story was spread widely among the Jews. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 28:11-15]</span>

That same day two of His disciples were going to a village called Emmaus, speaking together about all that had happened. Jesus Himself came near and began walking with them, but their eyes were kept from recognizing Him. He asked what they were discussing, and they told Him of Jesus of Nazareth, mighty in deed and word before God and all the people, and of the report that some women had seen a vision of angels who said He was alive. Beginning with Moses and all the Prophets, He explained to them the things concerning Himself in all the Scriptures. <span class="review-ref">[Luke 24:13-27]</span>

When they came near the village, He acted as though He would go farther. But they urged Him strongly to remain with them, because evening was drawing on. So He went in to stay with them. When He reclined at table, He took bread, blessed it, broke it, and gave it to them. Then their eyes were opened and they recognized Him, and He vanished from their sight. They said to one another that their hearts had been burning within them while He spoke on the road and opened the Scriptures to them. So they rose that same hour and returned to Jerusalem. <span class="review-ref">[Luke 24:28-35]</span>

That evening, while the doors were shut for fear of the authorities and while the disciples were speaking of these things, Jesus Himself stood among them and said, "Peace to you." They were startled and thought they were seeing a spirit, but He showed them His hands, His feet, and His side. He said, "Why are you troubled? Touch Me and see, for a spirit does not have flesh and bones as you see that I have." Then He ate before them. The disciples rejoiced when they saw the Lord. <span class="review-ref">[Luke 24:36-43; John 20:19-20]</span>

Then Jesus said to them again, "Peace to you. As the Father has sent Me, so I also send you." He breathed on them and said, "Receive the Holy Spirit." He opened their minds to understand the Scriptures and told them, "It is written that the Christ should suffer and rise from the dead on the third day, and that repentance for the forgiveness of sins should be proclaimed in His name to all nations, beginning from Jerusalem." <span class="review-ref">[Luke 24:44-49; John 20:21-23]</span>

Thomas, one of the Twelve, was not with them when Jesus came. When the others told him that they had seen the Lord, he said, "I will not believe unless I see and touch for myself." Eight days later the disciples were again inside, and Thomas was with them. Jesus came, though the doors were shut, stood among them, and said, "Peace to you." Then He told Thomas to put away his unbelief. Thomas answered, "My Lord and my God." Jesus said to him, "Because you have seen Me, you have believed. Blessed are those who have not seen and yet have believed." <span class="review-ref">[John 20:24-29]</span>

After this Jesus revealed Himself again to the disciples by the Sea of Tiberias. Simon Peter, Thomas, Nathanael, the sons of Zebedee, and two others had gone out to fish, but that night they caught nothing. At daybreak Jesus stood on the shore, though they did not know that it was He. He told them to cast the net on the right side of the boat, and they were no longer able to draw it in because of the multitude of fish. Then the disciple whom Jesus loved said, "It is the Lord." When they came ashore, they found a charcoal fire with fish and bread laid on it, and Jesus gave them breakfast. <span class="review-ref">[John 21:1-14]</span>

After they had eaten, Jesus said to Simon Peter, "Simon son of John, do you love Me more than these?" Peter answered, "Yes, Lord, You know that I love You." Jesus said, "Feed My lambs." Again and a third time He asked him, and Peter was grieved that He asked a third time. Then Jesus told him to tend His sheep and hinted at the death by which he would glorify God. When Peter asked about the beloved disciple, Jesus told him to follow Him without comparing his path to another's. <span class="review-ref">[John 21:15-23]</span>

The eleven disciples then went into Galilee, to the mountain where Jesus had directed them. When they saw Him, they worshiped Him, though some still hesitated. Jesus came and said, "All authority in heaven and on earth has been given to Me. Therefore go and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit, teaching them to keep all that I commanded you. And behold, I am with you always, to the end of the age." <span class="review-ref">[Matt 28:16-20]</span>

Later He led them out as far as Bethany. Lifting up His hands, He blessed them, and while He was blessing them He was taken up from them. They worshiped Him and returned to Jerusalem with great joy, and they were continually in the temple blessing God. Jesus had done many other signs in the presence of His disciples which are not written here, but these things were written so that men might believe that Jesus is the Christ, the Son of God, and that by believing they might have life in His name. <span class="review-ref">[Luke 24:50-53; John 20:30-31]</span>
