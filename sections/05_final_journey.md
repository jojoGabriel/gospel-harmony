# Final Journey to Jerusalem

References: Matthew 19-21; Mark 10-11; Luke 9:51-19:48; John 7-12

When the days were drawing near for Him to be taken up, Jesus set His face toward Jerusalem. He sent messengers ahead into a Samaritan village to make ready for Him, but they would not receive Him because He was going toward Jerusalem. When James and John saw it, they asked whether they should call down fire from heaven upon them, but He turned and rebuked them. So they went on to another village. <span class="review-ref">[Luke 9:51-56]</span>

After this He appointed seventy-two others and sent them ahead by twos into every town and place where He Himself was about to come. He told them to say, "Peace to this house," to heal the sick, and to declare that the kingdom of God had drawn near. They returned with joy, saying, "Lord, even the demons submit to us in Your name." He said to them, "Do not rejoice chiefly in this, that the spirits submit to you, but rejoice that your names are written in heaven." <span class="review-ref">[Luke 10:1-24]</span>

As they traveled, He entered a village where a woman named Martha received Him into her house. Her sister Mary sat at the Lord's feet and listened to His word, but Martha was troubled by much serving. When Martha asked that Mary be told to help her, Jesus answered, "Martha, Martha, you are anxious and troubled about many things, but one thing is needful. Mary has chosen the better part, and it will not be taken from her." <span class="review-ref">[Luke 10:38-42]</span>

The Feast of Tabernacles was near, and His brothers urged Him to go up to Judea and show Himself openly. Jesus told them, "My time has not yet fully come." After they had gone up, He also went, not openly, but quietly. In Jerusalem there was much whispering about Him among the crowds. Some said He was good, others that He was leading the people astray, yet none spoke freely because they feared the rulers. <span class="review-ref">[John 7:1-13]</span>

He went on His way through towns and villages, journeying toward Jerusalem. As He was passing between Samaria and Galilee, ten men with leprosy stood at a distance and cried out, "Jesus, Master, have mercy on us." He told them to go and show themselves to the priests, and as they went they were made clean. One of them, when he saw that he was healed, turned back, glorifying God with a loud voice, and he fell at Jesus' feet giving thanks. He was a Samaritan. Jesus said, "Were not ten cleansed? Where are the nine? Was none found to return and give glory to God except this foreigner?" Then He said to him, "Rise and go your way. Your faith has made you well." <span class="review-ref">[Luke 13:22; Luke 17:11-19]</span>

At the Feast of Dedication Jesus was walking in the temple, in Solomon's Colonnade. The rulers surrounded Him and pressed Him to speak plainly. When they again sought to seize Him, He escaped from their hands and went away beyond the Jordan to the place where John had first been baptizing. Many came to Him there and said, "John did no sign, but all that John said about this Man was true." And many believed in Him there. <span class="review-ref">[John 10:22-42]</span>

Now a certain man named Lazarus was sick, from Bethany, the village of Mary and her sister Martha. The sisters sent word to Jesus, but after hearing it He remained where He was two more days. Then He said to His disciples, "Let us go to Judea again." When He came near Bethany, Martha went out to meet Him and said, "Lord, if You had been here, my brother would not have died. Even now I know that whatever You ask from God, God will give You." Jesus said to her, "Your brother will rise again. I am the resurrection and the life. Whoever believes in Me, though he die, yet shall he live." Martha said, "Yes, Lord, I have believed that You are the Christ, the Son of God, who comes into the world." <span class="review-ref">[John 11:1-27]</span>

Then Mary came to Him, fell at His feet, and said, "Lord, if You had been here, my brother would not have died." When Jesus saw her weeping, and the Jews weeping with her, He was deeply moved in spirit and troubled. He said, "Where have you laid him?" They said, "Lord, come and see." Jesus wept. Then they came to the tomb, which was a cave with a stone lying against it. Jesus said, "Take away the stone." After Martha objected because her brother had been dead four days, Jesus lifted His eyes and gave thanks to the Father. Then He cried with a loud voice, "Lazarus, come out." The man who had died came out bound hand and foot with grave cloths, and Jesus said, "Loose him and let him go." <span class="review-ref">[John 11:28-44]</span>

Many of the Jews who had come to Mary believed in Him because they had seen what He did. But others went away and told the Pharisees. So the chief priests and the Pharisees gathered the council, and from that day they took counsel together to put Him to death. Therefore Jesus no longer walked openly among the Jews, but withdrew to a town called Ephraim near the wilderness and remained there with His disciples. <span class="review-ref">[John 11:45-57]</span>

Again crowds gathered to Him in the region beyond the Jordan, and He taught and healed among them. Little children were brought to Him that He might touch them, but He told His disciples not to hinder them, for the kingdom of God belongs to such as these. A ruler also came running and knelt before Him, but though he went away sorrowful, Jesus continued on the road with His disciples toward Jerusalem. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 19:13-30; Mark 10:13-31; Luke 18:15-30]</span>

As they were going up to Jerusalem, Jesus took the Twelve aside and said, "Behold, we are going up to Jerusalem, and all things written by the prophets concerning the Son of Man will be accomplished. He will be handed over, mocked, mistreated, killed, and on the third day rise." Yet they did not grasp these things. Along the way the sons of Zebedee sought places of honor, but He taught them that greatness in His kingdom belongs to the one who serves. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 20:17-28; Mark 10:32-45; Luke 18:31-34]</span>

When He came near Jericho, blind beggars cried out, "Lord, Son of David, have mercy on us." Though many tried to silence them, they cried out all the more. Jesus stood still and called for them. When they came near, He touched their eyes and said, "Receive your sight. Your faith has made you well." Immediately they received their sight and followed Him, glorifying God. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 20:29-34; Mark 10:46-52; Luke 18:35-43]</span>

Entering Jericho, He passed through the city, and there was a chief tax collector named Zacchaeus. Because he was small in stature, he ran ahead and climbed a sycamore tree to see Jesus. When Jesus came to the place, He looked up and said, "Zacchaeus, come down quickly, for today I must stay at your house." He hurried down and received Him joyfully. When others murmured, Jesus said, "Today salvation has come to this house, because he also is a son of Abraham. The Son of Man came to seek and to save what was lost." <span class="review-ref">[Luke 19:1-10]</span>

Six days before the Passover Jesus came to Bethany, where Lazarus was, whom He had raised from the dead. There they made Him a supper. Martha served, and Lazarus reclined with Him. Then Mary took costly perfume, anointed Jesus, and wiped His feet with her hair, so that the house was filled with the fragrance. When Judas objected, Jesus said, "Leave her alone. She has kept this for the day of My burial. The poor you always have with you, but you do not always have Me." Because of Lazarus also, the chief priests took counsel that they might kill him as well. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 26:6-13; Mark 14:3-9; John 12:1-11]</span>

The next day, when the great crowd heard that Jesus was coming to Jerusalem, He sent two disciples ahead to bring a colt on which no one had yet sat. They brought it to Him and spread garments upon it, and as He rode down from the Mount of Olives the crowds spread their cloaks and branches in the road. They cried out, "Hosanna to the Son of David! Blessed is the King who comes in the name of the Lord! Peace in heaven and glory in the highest!" Some Pharisees asked Him to rebuke His disciples, but He answered, "If these became silent, the stones themselves would cry out." <span class="review-ref">[Matt 21:1-11; Mark 11:1-10; Luke 19:28-40; John 12:12-19]</span>

When He drew near and saw the city, He wept over it and said, "If only you had known in this day the things that make for peace. But now they are hidden from your eyes." Then He entered Jerusalem and went into the temple courts. He drove out those who sold and bought there, overturned the tables, and said, "My house shall be a house of prayer, but you have made it a den of robbers." The blind and the lame came to Him in the temple, and He healed them. The chief priests and scribes were indignant, yet they could do nothing at that hour because all the people hung upon His words. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 21:12-17; Mark 11:11,15-19; Luke 19:41-48]</span>

As He went out from the city toward Bethany, He saw a fig tree in leaf but found no fruit on it, and He spoke against it. When they passed by again, the disciples saw that it had withered from the roots. Jesus taught them to have faith in God. By day He taught in the temple, and by night He went out and stayed on the mount called Olivet. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 21:18-22; Mark 11:12-14,20-26; Luke 21:37-38]</span>

Now certain Greeks who had come up to worship at the feast sought Him through Philip and Andrew. Jesus answered, "The hour has come for the Son of Man to be glorified. Unless a grain of wheat falls into the earth and dies, it remains alone." When He prayed that the Father would glorify His name, a voice came from heaven in answer. Though He had done so many signs before them, many still did not believe openly, for they loved the praise of men more than the praise of God. After crying out once more concerning faith, light, and judgment, Jesus departed and hid Himself from them. <span class="review-ref">[John 12:20-50]</span>
