# Teachings and Parables

References: Matthew 5-7; 11; 13; 18; 22-25; Mark 4; 9-10; 12-13; Luke 6; 10-12; 15-18; 20-21; John 7-10

When He saw the crowds, He went up the mountain, and after He sat down His disciples came to Him. Then He taught them, saying, "Blessed are the poor in spirit, because the kingdom of heaven is theirs. Blessed are those who mourn, because they will be comforted. Blessed are the meek, because they will inherit the earth. Blessed are those who hunger and thirst for righteousness, because they will be filled. Blessed are the merciful, because they will receive mercy. Blessed are the pure in heart, because they will see God. Blessed are the peacemakers, because they will be called sons of God. Blessed are those who are persecuted because of righteousness, because the kingdom of heaven is theirs. Blessed are you when men hate you, revile you, and speak every false evil against you on My account. Rejoice and be glad in that day, because your reward is great in heaven. But woe to you who are rich now, because you have already received your comfort. Woe to you who are full now, because you will hunger. Woe to you who laugh now, because you will mourn and weep.

You are the salt of the earth. But if salt becomes tasteless, how will its savor be restored? You are the light of the world. A city on a hill does not stay hidden, and a lamp is not lit in order to be put under a basket, but on a stand, so that it gives light to all in the house. Let your light shine before men, so that they may see your good works and glorify your Father in heaven. Do not think that I came to abolish the Law or the Prophets. I did not come to abolish them, but to fulfill them. Unless your righteousness goes beyond that of the scribes and Pharisees, you will not enter the kingdom of heaven." <span class="review-ref">[Matt 5:1-20; Luke 6:17-26]</span>

He continued, "You have heard that it was said to those of old, 'You shall not murder,' and that the murderer is liable to judgment. But I tell you that everyone who remains angry with his brother will answer for it. If you are bringing your gift to the altar and there remember that your brother has something against you, leave your gift there, first go and be reconciled to your brother, and then come and offer your gift.

You have heard that it was said, 'You shall not commit adultery.' But I tell you that whoever looks with desire has already committed adultery in his heart." <span class="review-ref">[Matt 5:21-48; Luke 6:27-38]</span>

He also said, "Again, you have heard that it was said, 'You shall not swear falsely.' But I tell you, do not swear by heaven or by earth. Let what you say be yes or no.

You have heard, 'Eye for eye and tooth for tooth.' But I tell you, do not return evil in kind. If anyone strikes you on one cheek, offer the other also. If anyone compels you to go one mile, go with him two. Give to the one who asks of you, and do not turn away from the one who would borrow from you.

You have heard, 'You shall love your neighbor.' But I tell you, love your enemies, do good to those who hate you, bless those who curse you, and pray for those who mistreat you, so that you may be sons of your Father, who causes His sun to rise on evil and good and sends rain on righteous and unrighteous alike." <span class="review-ref">[Matt 5:21-48; Luke 6:27-38]</span>

He also said, "When you give, do not do it before men in order to be seen by them. When you pray, do not stand to draw notice, but pray to your Father who is unseen. When you fast, do not put on a gloomy face so that men will notice it. The Father sees in secret.

Pray, then, in this way: 'Our Father in heaven, let Your name be hallowed. Let Your kingdom come. Let Your will be done, on earth as it is in heaven. Give us today the bread for this day. Forgive us our debts, as we also have forgiven our debtors. Do not lead us into trial, but deliver us from evil.'

Do not gather treasure for yourselves on earth, where moth and rust ruin and where thieves break in and steal. Gather treasure in heaven. Where your treasure is, there your heart will also be. The eye is the lamp of the body. No servant can serve two masters. Therefore do not be anxious about your life, what you will eat or drink, or about your body, what you will wear. Your Father knows that you need all these things. Seek first the kingdom of God and His righteousness, and these things will be given to you as well." <span class="review-ref">[Matt 6:1-34; Luke 11:1-4; Luke 12:22-31]</span>

He said further, "Do not judge, so that you may not be judged. With the measure you use, it will be measured back to you. Can the blind guide the blind? Will they not both fall into a pit? Why do you look at the speck in your brother's eye and fail to notice the beam in your own? First take the beam from your own eye, and then you will see clearly to take the speck from your brother's eye. Do not give what is holy to dogs, and do not throw your pearls before swine, lest they trample them.

Ask, and it will be given to you; seek, and you will find; knock, and the door will be opened to you. Which father among you, if his son asks for bread, will give him a stone? Or if he asks for a fish, will give him a serpent? Therefore whatever you would have men do to you, do the same to them, for this sums up the Law and the Prophets." <span class="review-ref">[Matt 7:1-29; Luke 6:39-49; Luke 11:9-13]</span>

Then He said, "Enter through the narrow gate. The gate is wide and the road broad that leads to destruction, and many go in by it. Beware of false prophets; you will know them by their fruit. Not everyone who says to Me, 'Lord, Lord,' will enter the kingdom of heaven, but the one who does My Father's will. Everyone who hears these words of Mine and acts on them is like a wise man who built his house on the rock. Rain fell, the floods came, and the winds beat against that house, but it did not fall, because it had been founded on the rock." <span class="review-ref">[Matt 7:1-29; Luke 6:39-49; Luke 11:9-13]</span>

At another time Jesus rejoiced and said, "I thank You, Father, Lord of heaven and earth, because You have hidden these things from the wise and understanding and revealed them to little children. Yes, Father, because this was pleasing in Your sight. All things have been handed over to Me by My Father, and no one fully knows the Son except the Father, nor the Father except the Son and anyone to whom the Son chooses to reveal Him. Come to Me, all who labor and are weighed down, and I will give you rest. Take My yoke upon you and learn from Me, because I am gentle and lowly in heart, and you will find rest for your souls. My yoke is easy to bear, and My burden is light." <span class="review-ref">[Matt 11:25-30]</span>

By the sea He taught the multitude in parables, saying, "A sower went out to sow. As he sowed, some seed fell along the path, and the birds came and devoured it. Some fell on rocky ground, where it sprang up quickly, but when the sun rose it withered because it had no depth of root. Some fell among thorns, and the thorns grew up and choked it. Some fell on good soil and brought forth fruit, some thirtyfold, some sixty, and some a hundred." Then He said, "He who has ears to hear, let him hear."

Again He said, "A lamp is not lit to be put under a basket or under a bed, but on a stand. The kingdom of God is like seed cast on the ground. A man sleeps and rises night and day, and the seed sprouts and grows, though he does not know how. The earth bears fruit by itself: first the blade, then the head, then the full grain in the head. To what shall we compare the kingdom of God? It is like a mustard seed, which is smaller than all the seeds when it is sown, yet when it has grown it becomes great. It is also like leaven, which a woman took and hid in flour until all was leavened." <span class="review-ref">[Matt 13:1-23, 31-35; Mark 4:1-34; Luke 8:4-18; Luke 13:18-21]</span>

To His disciples He also said, "The kingdom of heaven is like a man who sowed good seed in his field. But while men slept, his enemy came and sowed weeds among the wheat. Let both grow together until the harvest, and in the time of harvest the reapers will gather the weeds first for burning, but the wheat will be gathered into the barn. The field is the world, the good seed are the sons of the kingdom, the weeds are the sons of the evil one, and the harvest is the close of the age.

The kingdom of heaven is like treasure hidden in a field, which a man found and hid again, and in his joy he sold all he had and bought that field. Again, the kingdom is like a merchant seeking fine pearls; when he found one pearl of great worth, he went and sold all he had and bought it. Again, the kingdom is like a net cast into the sea gathering fish of every kind; when it was full, men drew it ashore and sorted the good from the bad. Therefore every scribe instructed for the kingdom is like a householder who brings out of his treasure what is new and what is old." <span class="review-ref">[Matt 13:24-30, 36-52]</span>

To the man who asked, "Who is my neighbor?" Jesus replied, "A man was going down from Jerusalem to Jericho and fell among robbers, who stripped him, wounded him, and left him half dead. A priest came that way and passed by. A Levite also saw him and passed by. But a Samaritan came to him, had compassion, bound up his wounds, set him on his own beast, and brought him to an inn to care for him." Then Jesus asked which of the three had proved neighbor to the man who fell among robbers. <span class="review-ref">[Luke 10:25-37]</span>

When tax collectors and sinners drew near to hear Him, and others murmured, He said, "What man among you, having a hundred sheep and losing one of them, would not leave the ninety-nine and go after the one that is lost until he finds it? And when he has found it, he lays it on his shoulders rejoicing. Or what woman, having ten silver coins and losing one, would not light a lamp and search carefully until she finds it? And when she has found it, she calls together her friends and neighbors to rejoice with her." <span class="review-ref">[Matt 18:12-14; Luke 15:1-10]</span>

He also said, "A man had two sons. The younger said, 'Father, give me the share of the estate that falls to me.' Not many days later he gathered everything and went into a distant country, and there he wasted his possessions in reckless living. But when he came to himself, he said, 'I will arise and go to my father.' While he was still far off, his father saw him, was moved with compassion, ran to him, embraced him, and kissed him. But the elder son was angry and would not go in, so his father came out and pleaded with him. It was fitting to rejoice, because this brother of yours was dead and lives again; he was lost and has been found." <span class="review-ref">[Luke 15:11-32]</span>

He taught His disciples to pray and not lose heart. He said, "Suppose one of you has a friend, and goes to him at midnight, saying, 'Friend, lend me three loaves.' Even if he will not rise because of friendship, he will rise because of persistence and give what is needed. So ask, and it will be given to you; seek, and you will find; knock, and the door will be opened. Which father among you, if his son asks for bread, will give him a stone?"

He also said, "In a certain city there was a judge who did not fear God or regard man. A widow kept coming to him, saying, 'Give me justice against my adversary.' Though he was unwilling for a time, afterward he said within himself that because this widow troubles him, he would give her justice." And He also said, "Two men went up to the temple to pray." One trusted in himself that he was righteous, but the other stood far off and cried, "God, be merciful to me, a sinner." The proud man did not go down justified; the humbled man did. He also took little children in His arms and said, "Let the little children come to Me, and do not hinder them, because the kingdom of God belongs to such as these." <span class="review-ref">[Luke 11:5-13; Luke 18:1-17]</span>

He warned them, "Guard yourselves from every kind of covetousness, because a man's life does not consist in the abundance of what he possesses." Then He said, "The land of a rich man produced plentifully, and he said to himself, 'I will tear down my barns and build larger ones, and there I will store all my grain and goods.' But God said to him, 'Fool, this night your soul is required of you.'" He also said, "Be dressed and ready, and keep your lamps burning. Blessed is that servant whom his master finds watching. From everyone to whom much is given, much will be required." <span class="review-ref">[Matt 6:19-24; Luke 12:13-48; Luke 16:1-31]</span>

Again He said, "No servant can serve two masters." He also spoke of a steward who acted shrewdly because he knew an accounting was coming, and He set before them a rich man clothed in purple and fine linen while Lazarus lay poor at his gate. The rich man received good things in his lifetime, but afterward found torment, while Lazarus was comforted. So He taught them to be faithful with what passes away, because where their treasure is, there their heart will also be. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 6:19-24; Luke 12:13-48; Luke 16:1-31]</span>

Among His disciples He said, "If anyone would be first, he must become last of all and servant of all." He set a little child in their midst and said, "Unless you turn and become like little children, you will never enter the kingdom of heaven. Whoever humbles himself like this child is the greatest in the kingdom of heaven." He warned them, "Whoever causes one of these little ones who believe in Me to stumble will answer for it. If your hand or your foot causes you to stumble, cut it off; it is better to enter life maimed than to be cast away whole." He taught them, "If your brother sins, reprove him; and if he repents, forgive him." When Peter asked how often he must forgive, Jesus answered, "I do not tell you merely seven times, but again and again beyond counting." <span class="review-ref">[Matt 18:1-35; Mark 9:33-50; Mark 10:1-31; Luke 9:46-50; Luke 17:1-10]</span>

He also answered concerning marriage, "From the beginning God made them male and female. Therefore what God has joined together, let not man separate." When a ruler asked what he must do to inherit eternal life, Jesus said, "You know the commandments." The man said, "All these I have done from my youth." Then Jesus said, "One thing is still lacking. Sell what you have, give to the poor, and you will have treasure in heaven; then come, follow Me." But the man went away sorrowful, because he had many possessions. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 18:1-35; Mark 9:33-50; Mark 10:1-31; Luke 9:46-50; Luke 17:1-10]</span>

When the feasts came and He went up to Jerusalem, Jesus taught openly in the temple though His enemies watched for an opportunity against Him. He said, "My teaching is not Mine, but His who sent Me. If any man desires to do His will, he will know whether this teaching is from God. Do not judge by appearances, but judge with right judgment." And on the great day of the feast He cried out, "If any man is thirsty, let him come to Me and drink. Whoever believes in Me, rivers of living water will flow from within him." Some said, "This is truly the Prophet." Others said, "This is the Christ." Yet many stumbled at Him because they judged by appearances. <span class="review-ref">[John 7:1-52]</span>

There He also said, "I am the light of the world. Whoever follows Me will not walk in darkness, but will have the light of life." He said, "If you remain in My word, you are truly My disciples, and you will know the truth, and the truth will make you free." When they answered, "We have never been slaves to anyone," Jesus said, "Everyone who practices sin is a slave of sin. If the Son sets you free, you will be truly free." When many claimed Abraham, He said, "If you were Abraham's children, you would do Abraham's works." At the last He said, "Your father Abraham rejoiced to see My day. Before Abraham came to be, I am." <span class="review-ref">[John 8:12-59]</span>

After He gave sight to the man born blind, Jesus said, "For judgment I came into this world, so that those who do not see may see, and those who think they see may become blind." Then He said, "He who enters by the door is the shepherd of the sheep. He calls his own sheep by name and leads them out." He also said, "I am the door for the sheep," and again, "I am the good shepherd. The good shepherd lays down his life for the sheep." At the Feast of Dedication He said, "My works bear witness to Me. My sheep hear My voice, and I know them, and they follow Me. I give them eternal life, and no one will snatch them from My hand or from My Father's hand." Some took up stones again, while others remembered John's witness and believed in Him. <span class="review-ref">[John 9:1-41; John 10:1-42]</span>

In the temple courts and before hostile questioners, Jesus said, "Show Me the coin for the tax. Whose image and inscription does it bear?" When they answered, He said, "Then render to Caesar what bears Caesar's claim, and to God what belongs to God." To those who denied the resurrection He said, "The God of Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob is not God of the dead, but of the living." When He was asked about the greatest commandment, He answered, "The first is to love the Lord your God with all your heart, soul, mind, and strength, and the second is like it: to love your neighbor as yourself." Then He warned the people, "Beware of the scribes," for they loved honor and burdened others. He also watched a poor widow put in two small coins, and said, "This poor widow has given more than all the rest, because she gave out of her poverty all she had." <span class="review-ref">[Matt 22:15-46; Matt 23:1-39; Mark 12:13-44; Luke 20:20-47]</span>

As the end drew nearer, He said, "See that no one leads you astray. Many will come in My name, and you will hear of wars and tumults, but do not be terrified. Then they will see the Son of Man coming with power and great glory. Stay awake, and pray that you may have strength to stand before the Son of Man. Watch therefore, because you do not know the day or the hour."

Then He said, "The kingdom of heaven will be like ten virgins who took their lamps and went out to meet the bridegroom. Five were wise and five were foolish." Again He said, "It is like a man going on a journey, who entrusted his possessions to his servants." The faithful servants entered their master's joy, but the fearful and slothful servant lost what had been entrusted to him. At the last He set before them the Son of Man coming in glory and all the nations gathered before Him, and He said, "He will separate them as a shepherd separates sheep from goats." To those at His right He would say, "Come, you who are blessed of My Father, inherit the kingdom prepared for you." Those who refused mercy would go away to judgment, but the righteous into life. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 24:1-25:46; Mark 13:1-37; Luke 21:5-36]</span>
