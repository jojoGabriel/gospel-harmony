# Prologue: The Word Before All Things

References: John 1:1-18

At the beginning the Word already was. The Word was with God, and the Word was God. He was with God at the beginning. Through Him all things came to be, and apart from Him not one thing came to be. In Him was life, and that life was the light of men. The light shines in the darkness, and the darkness has not overcome it. <span class="review-ref">[John 1:1-5]</span>

There was a man sent from God whose name was John. He came as a witness, to bear witness concerning the Light, so that through him men might believe. He was not the Light, but came to bear witness concerning the Light. <span class="review-ref">[John 1:6-8]</span>

The true Light, which gives light to every man, was coming into the world. He was in the world, and though the world came to be through Him, the world did not know Him. He came to His own, and His own did not receive Him. But to all who did receive Him, to those believing in His name, He gave the right to become children of God, born not of blood, nor of the will of the flesh, nor of the will of man, but of God. <span class="review-ref">[John 1:9-13]</span>

And the Word became flesh and dwelt among us, and we saw His glory, glory as of the Father's only Son, full of grace and truth. John bore witness concerning Him and cried out, "This is the One of whom I said, 'He who comes after me has gone before me, because He was before me.'" <span class="review-ref">[John 1:14-15]</span>

From His fullness we have all received, even grace upon grace. The law was given through Moses; grace and truth came through Jesus Christ. No one has ever seen God; the only Son, who is at the Father's side, has made Him known. <span class="review-ref">[John 1:16-18]</span>
