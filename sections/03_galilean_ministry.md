# Galilean Ministry

References: Matthew 4-18; Mark 1-9; Luke 4-9; John 4-6

As He went northward, Jesus had to pass through Samaria, and He came to a town called Sychar, near the plot of ground Jacob had given to his son Joseph. Wearied from the journey, He sat by the well about the sixth hour while His disciples had gone into the town to buy food. A Samaritan woman came to draw water, and Jesus said to her, "Give Me a drink." The woman said, "How is it that You, being a Jew, ask a drink from me, a Samaritan woman?" Jesus answered, "If you knew the gift of God, and who it is that says to you, 'Give Me a drink,' you would have asked Him, and He would have given you living water." <span class="review-ref">[John 4:4-10]</span>

The woman said, "Sir, You have nothing to draw with and the well is deep. From where, then, do You get this living water? Are You greater than our father Jacob, who gave us the well and drank from it himself?" Jesus answered, "Everyone who drinks of this water will thirst again, but whoever drinks of the water I give him will never thirst. The water I give will become in him a spring of water rising up to eternal life." The woman said, "Sir, give me this water, so that I may not thirst or keep coming here to draw." <span class="review-ref">[John 4:11-15]</span>

Jesus said to her, "Go, call your husband, and come here." The woman answered, "I have no husband." Jesus said, "You have spoken rightly, for you have had five husbands, and the man you now have is not your husband." The woman said, "Sir, I see that You are a prophet. Our fathers worshiped on this mountain, but you Jews say that Jerusalem is the place where men ought to worship." Jesus answered, "Believe Me, woman, the hour is coming when you will worship the Father neither on this mountain nor in Jerusalem. The hour is coming, and now is, when the true worshipers will worship the Father in spirit and truth, for the Father seeks such people to worship Him." <span class="review-ref">[John 4:16-24]</span>

The woman said, "I know that Messiah is coming, and when He comes, He will tell us all things." Jesus said to her, "I who speak to you am He." At that moment His disciples returned and marveled that He was speaking with a woman, yet no one said what he sought or why He was speaking with her. Then the woman left her water jar, went back into the town, and said to the people, "Come, see a Man who told me all that I ever did. Could this be the Christ?" And they went out of the town and were coming to Him. <span class="review-ref">[John 4:25-30]</span>

Meanwhile the disciples urged Him to eat, but He said, "I have food to eat that you do not know about." When they wondered whether someone had brought Him food, Jesus said, "My food is to do the will of the One who sent Me and to finish His work. Lift up your eyes and look at the fields, for they are already white for harvest. The reaper is already receiving wages and gathering fruit for eternal life, so that sower and reaper may rejoice together." <span class="review-ref">[John 4:31-38]</span>

Many Samaritans from that town believed in Him because of the woman's testimony, that He had told her all that she had ever done. So when the Samaritans came to Him, they asked Him to stay with them, and He stayed there two days. And many more believed because of His word. They said to the woman, "It is no longer because of your speaking that we believe, for we have heard for ourselves and know that this One truly is the Savior of the world." <span class="review-ref">[John 4:39-42]</span>

After that He went into Galilee, teaching in their synagogues, and He was glorified by all. He came again to Cana, where He had made the water wine, and there He healed the son of a royal official at Capernaum, speaking the word from a distance and causing the child to live at that very hour. From there He went to Nazareth, where He had been brought up, and as was His custom He entered the synagogue on the Sabbath and stood up to read. The scroll of Isaiah was handed to Him, and He read of the One anointed by the Spirit to preach good news to the poor, proclaim liberty to the captives, recovery of sight to the blind, and the year of the Lord's favor. Then He sat down and said, "Today this Scripture has been fulfilled in your hearing." <span class="review-ref">[John 4:43-54; Luke 4:16-21]</span>

At first all spoke well of Him and marveled at the gracious words that came from His mouth. But He said, "Truly I tell you, no prophet is accepted in his own country. There were many widows in Israel in the days of Elijah, yet Elijah was sent to none of them, but to a widow in Sidon. There were many lepers in Israel in the time of Elisha, yet none of them was cleansed, but only Naaman the Syrian." At this those in the synagogue were filled with wrath. They rose up, drove Him out of the town, and led Him to the brow of the hill in order to throw Him down. But He passed through their midst and went on His way. <span class="review-ref">[Luke 4:22-30]</span>

Then He came and settled in Capernaum by the sea, in the region of Zebulun and Naphtali, and from that time He continued preaching, "Repent, for the kingdom of heaven is at hand." Walking by the Sea of Galilee, He saw Simon and Andrew casting a net into the sea, for they were fishermen. He said to them, "Follow Me, and I will make you fishers of men." They left their nets and followed Him. Going on from there, He saw James and John, the sons of Zebedee, with their father in the boat mending their nets, and He called them also. They immediately left the boat and their father and followed Him. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 4:13-22; Mark 1:16-20]</span>

Once, while the multitude pressed upon Him to hear the word of God, He saw two boats by the lake, and the fishermen had gone out of them and were washing their nets. Getting into Simon's boat, He asked him to put out a little from the land, and He sat down and taught the multitude from the boat. When He had finished speaking, He told Simon, "Put out into deep water, and let down your nets for a catch." Simon answered, "Master, we have toiled all night and taken nothing, but at Your word I will let down the nets." When they had done so, they enclosed such a great number of fish that their nets began to break. Simon Peter fell down at Jesus' knees and said, "Depart from me, Lord, for I am a sinful man." But Jesus said to Simon, "Do not be afraid; from now on you will catch men." So they brought the boats to land, left everything, and followed Him. <span class="review-ref">[Luke 5:1-11]</span>

He entered Capernaum, and on the Sabbath He taught in the synagogue. The people were astonished at His teaching, for His word carried authority. In the synagogue there was a man with an unclean spirit, and Jesus rebuked the spirit, and it came out of the man without harming him. Amazement came upon them all, and news about Him spread into every place in the surrounding region. Leaving the synagogue, He entered Simon's house and rebuked Simon's mother-in-law's fever, and it left her. At sunset they brought to Him all who were sick or oppressed by demons, and He laid His hands on many and healed them. Very early the next morning He departed to a desolate place, but when the crowds sought Him, He said, "I must preach the good news of the kingdom of God to the other towns also, for that is why I was sent." <span class="review-ref">[Mark 1:21-39; Luke 4:31-44; Matt 8:14-17]</span>

A leper came to Him, falling on his knees and saying, "Lord, if You are willing, You can make me clean." Moved with compassion, Jesus stretched out His hand, touched him, and said, "I am willing; be cleansed." Immediately the leprosy left him. Yet the report about Him spread all the more, and great multitudes came to hear Him and to be healed of their infirmities. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 8:1-4; Mark 1:40-45; Luke 5:12-16]</span>

When He returned to Capernaum after some days, it was reported that He was at home, and so many gathered that there was no more room, not even at the door. Then men came bringing a paralytic, carried by four of them, and when they could not reach Jesus because of the crowd, they uncovered the roof above Him and lowered the mat on which the paralytic lay. Seeing their faith, Jesus said to the man, "Son, your sins are forgiven." Some of the scribes reasoned in their hearts that He was blaspheming, for who can forgive sins but God alone. Jesus, knowing in His spirit what they were thinking, asked whether it was easier to say, "Your sins are forgiven," or, "Rise, take up your mat and walk." Then He told the paralytic to rise, take up his mat, and go to his house. Immediately he rose and went out before them all, and they glorified God. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 9:1-8; Mark 2:1-12; Luke 5:17-26]</span>

After this Jesus saw a tax collector named Levi, called Matthew, sitting at the tax booth, and He said to him, "Follow Me." Leaving everything, he rose and followed Him. Levi made a great feast for Him in his house, and many tax collectors and others were reclining at table with them. When the Pharisees and their scribes complained that He ate with tax collectors and sinners, Jesus answered, "Those who are well have no need of a physician, but those who are sick. I have not come to call the righteous, but sinners, to repentance." <span class="review-ref">[Matt 9:9-13; Mark 2:13-17; Luke 5:27-32]</span>

At another time He healed a man at the pool of Bethesda who had been afflicted for many years, saying to him, "Rise, take up your mat, and walk." Because this was done on the Sabbath, the Jews persecuted Him all the more. He also defended His disciples when they plucked heads of grain on the Sabbath, and He healed a man with a withered hand in the synagogue, saying, "The Son of Man is Lord even of the Sabbath," and asking whether it is lawful on the Sabbath to do good or to do harm, to save life or to kill. From that time the Pharisees watched Him with growing hatred and took counsel against Him. <span class="review-ref">[John 5:1-18; Matt 12:1-14; Mark 2:23-3:6; Luke 6:1-11]</span>

Great multitudes from Galilee, Judea, Jerusalem, Idumea, beyond the Jordan, and the region of Tyre and Sidon came to Him. He withdrew often to the mountain to pray, and from among His disciples He chose twelve, whom He named apostles: Simon, whom He called Peter, and Andrew his brother; James and John; Philip and Bartholomew; Matthew and Thomas; James the son of Alphaeus and Simon called the Zealot; Judas the son of James; and Judas Iscariot, who became a traitor. <span class="review-ref">[Mark 3:7-19; Luke 6:12-16]</span>

He continued teaching in the synagogues, proclaiming the kingdom, and healing every disease and sickness among the people. In Capernaum He marveled at a centurion's faith and healed his servant from afar. At Nain He met a funeral procession for the only son of a widow, and He touched the bier and commanded the young man to arise, and He gave him back to his mother. Women who had been healed of evil spirits and infirmities also followed Him and ministered to Him out of their means. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 8:5-13; Luke 7:1-17; Luke 8:1-3]</span>

When John heard in prison about the works of the Christ, he sent word asking whether Jesus was the One who was to come, or whether they should look for another. Jesus said, "Go and tell John what you have seen and heard: the blind receive sight, the lame walk, lepers are cleansed, the deaf hear, the dead are raised, and the poor have good news preached to them. Blessed is the one who does not stumble because of Me." Then He bore witness to John before the people, saying, "What did you go out into the wilderness to see? A reed shaken by the wind? Among those born of women there has not arisen a greater than John the Baptist." <span class="review-ref">[Matt 11:2-15; Luke 7:18-28]</span>

Crowds gathered around Him beside the sea and in the villages, and He spoke many things to them in parables. Yet He also took His disciples aside and taught them privately. He calmed a great storm on the sea with a word, so that those in the boat marveled that even the winds and the waves obeyed Him. On the other side He delivered a man from a legion of demons, and the man who had been possessed proclaimed throughout the Decapolis what great things Jesus had done for him. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 13:1-3; Matt 8:23-34; Mark 4:1-41; Mark 5:1-20; Luke 8:4-39]</span>

When He returned, Jairus, a ruler of the synagogue, fell at His feet and pleaded for his little daughter, who was dying. As Jesus went, a woman who had suffered from a flow of blood for twelve years touched the fringe of His garment, believing she would be healed, and immediately she was made well. Then Jesus went to Jairus's house, took the child by the hand, and raised her up. In many places He healed the blind, the mute, and those brought in weakness to Him, and the crowds marveled, saying, "Nothing like this has ever been seen in Israel." <span class="review-ref">[Matt 9:18-34; Mark 5:21-43; Luke 8:40-56]</span>

Though He did many mighty works, not all believed. Some accused Him of casting out demons by the ruler of demons, but He answered, "Every kingdom divided against itself is brought to ruin. If Satan is divided against himself, how will his kingdom stand?" He warned them against speaking against the Holy Spirit. Others sought a sign from heaven, but He said, "No sign will be given to this generation except the sign of Jonah." Even in His own region many stumbled at Him, and because of their unbelief He did not do many mighty works there. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 12:22-45; Matt 13:53-58; Mark 6:1-6; Luke 11:14-23, 29-32]</span>

Then He called the Twelve to Himself and began to send them out, giving them authority over unclean spirits and power to preach the kingdom of God, heal the sick, and call men to repentance. He charged them, "Take nothing for the journey beyond what is needed, and wherever you are received, remain there until you depart. Freely you have received; freely give." <span class="review-ref">[Matt 10:1-15; Mark 6:7-13; Luke 9:1-6]</span>

When Herod heard of all that was happening, he was perplexed, and some were saying, "John the Baptist has risen from the dead." For Herod had arrested John and bound him because of Herodias, his brother's wife, for John had said to him, "It is not lawful for you to have her." On Herod's birthday the daughter of Herodias danced before the guests and pleased him, and at her mother's urging she asked for the head of John the Baptist on a platter. Though the king was distressed, because of his oaths and his dinner guests he commanded it to be given her. John was beheaded in the prison, and his disciples came, took away the body, buried it, and brought Jesus the news. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 14:1-12; Mark 6:14-29; Luke 9:7-9]</span>

When the apostles returned from preaching, Jesus took them aside to a solitary place near Bethsaida. But the crowds learned of it and followed Him on foot from the towns. He welcomed them, spoke to them concerning the kingdom of God, and healed those who needed healing. As evening approached, His disciples urged Him to send the crowd away, but Jesus said, "You give them something to eat." Taking the five loaves and two fish, He looked up to heaven, blessed and broke them, and gave them to the disciples to set before the multitude. All ate and were satisfied, and twelve baskets full of broken pieces were taken up. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 14:13-21; Mark 6:30-44; Luke 9:10-17; John 6:1-13]</span>

When the people saw the sign He had performed, they said, "This is truly the Prophet who is to come into the world." Jesus, knowing they meant to seize Him and make Him king by force, withdrew alone to the mountain. That evening His disciples went down to the sea and set out in the boat. In the last watch of the night Jesus came toward them, walking on the water. They were frightened, but He said, "Take courage. It is I. Do not be afraid." Peter answered, "Lord, if it is You, command me to come to You on the water." Jesus said, "Come." But when Peter saw the wind, fear took hold of him and he began to sink, and Jesus reached out and took hold of him. When they entered the boat, it came at once to the shore, and those with Him worshiped Him, saying, "Truly You are the Son of God." <span class="review-ref">[Matt 14:22-33; Mark 6:45-52; John 6:14-21]</span>

The next day the crowds searched for Him because they had eaten the loaves and been filled. Jesus said to them, "Do not labor for the food that perishes, but for the food that endures to eternal life, which the Son of Man will give you." They said to Him, "Sir, always give us this bread." Jesus said, "I am the bread of life. Whoever comes to Me will not hunger, and whoever believes in Me will never thirst. The bread of God is the One who comes down from heaven and gives life to the world, and the bread that I will give for the life of the world is My flesh." When many murmured at this, they said, "This word is hard. Who can hear it?" Jesus did not draw back from it. So many of His disciples turned away and no longer walked with Him. Then Jesus said to the Twelve, "Do you also wish to go away?" Simon Peter answered, "Lord, to whom would we go? You have the words of eternal life, and we have believed and come to know that You are the Holy One of God." <span class="review-ref">[John 6:22-71]</span>

After this the Pharisees and scribes from Jerusalem found fault with His disciples for eating with defiled hands, but Jesus answered, "A man is defiled not by what enters from outside, but by what proceeds from the heart." Then He withdrew to the region of Tyre and Sidon. There a Canaanite woman, a Syrophoenician by birth, cried out, "Have mercy on me, Lord, Son of David. My daughter is grievously tormented by a demon." Though He tested her faith and said, "It is not right to take the children's bread and throw it to the dogs," she answered, "Yes, Lord, yet even the little dogs eat the crumbs that fall from their masters' table." Then Jesus said, "O woman, great is your faith. Let it be done for you as you desire," and her daughter was healed from that hour. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 15:1-28; Mark 7:1-30]</span>

Returning by way of Sidon to the Sea of Galilee, through the Decapolis, Jesus healed a man who was deaf and had difficulty speaking, and the people marveled, saying, "He has done all things well. He makes even the deaf hear and the mute speak." Again a large crowd stayed with Him for three days, and He fed about four thousand men, besides women and children, with seven loaves and a few small fish. Afterward the Pharisees demanded a sign from heaven, but Jesus said to His disciples, "Beware the leaven of the Pharisees and Sadducees." Then at Bethsaida He took a blind man by the hand and restored his sight. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 15:29-16:12; Mark 7:31-8:26]</span>

Later, when Jesus was praying alone with His disciples nearby, He asked, "Who do the crowds say that I am?" They answered, "Some say John the Baptist, others Elijah, and others one of the prophets." Then He asked, "But who do you yourselves say that I am?" Simon Peter answered, "You are the Christ of God, the Son of the living God." Jesus declared him blessed for that confession and from that time began to teach His disciples openly, saying, "The Son of Man must suffer many things, be rejected by the elders, chief priests, and scribes, be killed, and on the third day rise." He also said, "If anyone wishes to come after Me, let him deny himself, take up his cross each day, and follow Me." <span class="review-ref">[Matt 16:13-28; Mark 8:27-38; Luke 9:18-27]</span>

About eight days after these sayings, Jesus took Peter, John, and James up the mountain to pray. While He was praying, the appearance of His face was altered, and His clothing became white and dazzling. Moses and Elijah appeared in glory and spoke with Him about the departure He was soon to accomplish at Jerusalem. Then a bright cloud overshadowed them, and a voice came from the cloud, saying, "This is My Son, whom I have chosen. Hear Him." At this the disciples fell on their faces in fear. Jesus came to them, touched them, and told them to rise and not be afraid. As they came down the mountain, He instructed them to tell no one what they had seen until the Son of Man had risen from the dead. <span class="review-ref">[Matt 17:1-13; Mark 9:2-13; Luke 9:28-36]</span>

When they came down to the crowd, a man cried out concerning his son, who was tormented by an unclean spirit. The disciples had been unable to cast it out, but Jesus rebuked the spirit and healed the boy. All were astonished at the majesty of God, yet the disciples did not understand when He again told them, "The Son of Man is about to be delivered into the hands of men." <span class="review-ref">[Matt 17:14-23; Mark 9:14-32; Luke 9:37-45]</span>
